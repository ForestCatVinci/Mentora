WHAT IS MENTORIA HUB?
----------------------
Mentoria Hub is an all-in-one digital platform for high school students in
Kazakhstan and the CIS region (grades 8–11) who want to build a strong future
for themselves — whether that means getting into a top university abroad,
winning olympiads, launching a startup, or doing research.

The problem it solves: promising students in Kazakhstan have no single place
where they can discover opportunities (competitions, scholarships, summer
schools, internships), learn from structured courses, connect with mentors,
and get personalized guidance — all at once. Information is scattered across
Telegram channels, social media, and word-of-mouth. Students miss deadlines,
don't know what's relevant for their grade, and have no roadmap.

Mentoria Hub fixes all of that in one product.

WHO IS IT FOR?
================================================================================

PRIMARY USER: STUDENT (grades 8–11)
  - Discovers opportunities filtered to their grade and interests
  - Follows a grade-specific roadmap of what to do this year
  - Takes structured courses (like Udemy, but for school-age students)
  - Saves deadlines to a personal calendar
  - Chats with an AI assistant that knows their profile and the platform
  - Connects with real mentors

SECONDARY USER: MENTOR
  - University students or professionals who want to guide younger students
  - Can publish posts about opportunities, events, scholarships
  - Can create and publish full video courses with sections and lessons
  - Has a profile page with contacts (Telegram, phone, email)
  - Accessible through a special invite code (6767)

CORE FEATURES — WHAT THE PRODUCT ACTUALLY DOES
================================================================================

1. PERSONALIZED ONBOARDING
   When a student registers, they go through a 3-step setup:
   - Pick their grade (8 / 9 / 10 / 11)
   - Pick their interest areas (STEM, Business, CS, Math, Research,
     Olympiads, Entrepreneurship, Science, Social Impact, Language)
   - Pick their goals (Study abroad / Olympiads / Startup / Research)
   This data is stored in their profile and used to personalize everything.

2. SMART FEED
   The main page shows a personalized feed of posts — opportunities, events,
   announcements — ranked by relevance to the student's interests and grade.
   Each post card shows: title, image, category, deadline, description, and
   tags. Students can save any post with one click.

3. OPPORTUNITIES CATALOG
   A filterable catalog of all available opportunities:
   - Filter by category: Olympiad, Scholarship, Summer School, Internship,
     Grant, Competition, Event, Course, Other
   - Filter by grade: 8 / 9 / 10 / 11 (shows only relevant posts)
   - Full-text search across title, description, and tags
   - All filtering is instant (client-side, no loading)
   - Each post can be saved/bookmarked

4. COURSES (Udemy-style learning)
   Mentors and staff can build full structured courses:
   - Course has title, description, image, category, difficulty level
     (beginner / intermediate / advanced)
   - Inside each course: sections (chapters) → lessons (videos)
   - Each lesson has a title and a YouTube video link
   - Students enroll in a course, watch lessons, and mark them complete
   - Progress is tracked per lesson (checkmark system)
   - Unenrolled students see locked lessons (lock icon)
   - Mentors use a visual course editor to build curriculum inline

5. PERSONAL CALENDAR
   - Students save posts that have deadlines or event date ranges
   - The calendar page shows a full monthly view
   - Red dot on a day = there's a deadline that day
   - Blue dot on a day = a period event spans that day
   - Click any day to see what's happening
   - Helps students never miss an important deadline

6. GRADE ROADMAP
   A visual timeline for all 4 years of high school (grades 8–11):
   - Each grade has curated action items: what competitions to enter,
     what skills to build, what exams to prepare for
   - The student's current grade is highlighted
   - Each item is a checkbox — students tick off what they've done
   - Progress is saved locally
   - Examples: "Register for math olympiad", "Take IELTS (target 7.0+)",
     "Apply for Bolashak / Chevening scholarship"

7. AI CHATBOT — "MENTORA"
   A floating chat assistant powered by GPT-4o-mini that:
   - Knows the student's profile: name, grade, interests, goals
   - Knows all currently published opportunities and their deadlines
   - Knows all available courses on the platform
   - Responds in the language the student writes in (Russian, Kazakh, English)
   - Keeps conversation history (last 12 messages for context)
   - Streams responses word-by-word (no waiting for full reply)
   - Has quick suggestion chips: "Покажи ближайшие дедлайны",
     "Что делать в 10 классе?", "Посоветуй курс"
   - Always gives personalized answers based on the student's actual profile
   The chatbot is not a generic ChatGPT wrapper — it's trained on the
   platform's real data and responds with specific opportunity names,
   deadlines, and course recommendations from the actual catalog.

8. MENTOR DIRECTORY
   - Browse all registered mentors on the platform
   - Each mentor has a profile: name, university, speciality, bio
   - Contact options: Telegram, phone, email
   - Students can reach out directly

9. STUDENT DASHBOARD
   - Shows saved posts (the student's personal bookmarks)
   - Can unsave posts
   - Manage personal profile settings


================================================================================
TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

FRONTEND
  - React 18 + TypeScript + Vite
  - Tailwind CSS (custom design system with primary color tokens)
  - React Router v6 (SPA with nested routes)
  - Deployed on Vercel
  - Mobile-responsive with bottom navigation bar

BACKEND
  - Python 3.12 + FastAPI
  - Streaming SSE endpoint for AI chatbot (real-time word-by-word output)
  - RESTful API: /auth, /posts, /courses, /feed, /mentors, /chat
  - Deployed on Fly.io (Amsterdam region, Docker container, 256MB RAM)
  - Auto-starts on traffic, auto-stops when idle (serverless-like behavior)

DATABASE & AUTH
  - Supabase (hosted PostgreSQL)
  - Supabase Auth for email/password authentication
  - Row-Level Security (RLS) enabled in DB; backend uses service_role key
    to bypass RLS for trusted server-side operations
  - Tables: users/profiles, posts, saved_posts, courses, sections, lessons,
    enrollments, user_progress

AI
  - OpenAI GPT-4o-mini
  - Custom system prompt built dynamically per request using the student's
    profile + live data fetched from Supabase at request time
  - Conversation history passed with each request (last 12 messages)
  - Server-Sent Events (SSE) for streaming output to the browser

USER ROLES
  - student — default role after registration
  - mentor — unlocked via special invite code (6767) at registration
  - staff / admin — platform operators who can post and manage content

BUSINESS MODEL & COMMERCIAL POTENTIAL
================================================================================

THE MARKET
  Kazakhstan alone has ~700,000 high school students in grades 8–11.
  Across CIS (Russia, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, Azerbaijan, etc.) the
  addressable market is in the tens of millions of students.

  None of the existing platforms solve this problem completely:
  - Yandex Uchebnik / Foxford: general school curriculum, no opportunities
  - Telegram channels: no structure, no personalization, no tracking
  - Khan Academy: US-focused, no local context, no opportunity discovery
  - LinkedIn: not designed for 14–17 year olds, no structured learning

REVENUE STREAMS (how the platform makes money)

  1. PREMIUM SUBSCRIPTION (B2C)
     Students pay a monthly fee (e.g., $5–10/month) for:
     - Access to premium courses
     - AI chatbot with unlimited usage
     - Priority notifications for new opportunities
     - Advanced calendar features and deadline reminders
     Free tier: feed + basic catalog browsing

  2. COURSE MARKETPLACE (B2C/B2B)
     Mentors and experts sell courses on the platform.
     Mentoria Hub takes a percentage (e.g., 30%) of each course sale.
     This creates a marketplace model similar to Udemy.

  3. OPPORTUNITY LISTINGS (B2B)
     Organizations (universities, NGOs, government programs, companies)
     pay to feature their opportunities prominently in the catalog and feed.
     Targeted reach to students who actually match their requirements.

  4. INSTITUTIONAL LICENSING (B2B)
     Schools and lyceums pay for a white-labeled version of the platform
     to use internally — giving their students access to the full platform
     with the school's branding and curated content.

  5. MENTOR MARKETPLACE (future)
     Paid 1-on-1 mentoring sessions booked through the platform.
     Mentoria Hub takes a platform fee on each booking.

UNIT ECONOMICS (rough estimates)
  - Cost to acquire one student: low (organic via school partnerships,
    word-of-mouth, social media)
  - Monthly subscription revenue per paying user: $5–10
  - With 10,000 paying users: $50,000–$100,000 MRR
  - Marginal cost per user is very low (serverless infra, AI cost ~$0.01
    per conversation at GPT-4o-mini pricing)

TRACTION POTENTIAL
  - Platform is fully functional and live today
  - Can onboard schools as early customers immediately
  - Kazakhstan government has active digitalization programs in education
    (potential for grants and government contracts)
  - Bolashak, DAAD, Chevening — scholarship programs that would pay to
    reach qualified students directly

WHAT MAKES IT DIFFERENT (THE PITCH)
================================================================================

1. NOT JUST A CATALOG — it's personalized. The feed, the AI, the roadmap all
   adapt to WHO the student is: their grade, their interests, their goals.

2. END-TO-END — from "I don't know what to do" to "I applied and got in":
   discover → learn → track → connect → apply. One platform.

3. LOCAL CONTEXT — designed specifically for Kazakh/CIS students.
   It knows about Bolashak, NIS, olympiad seasons, local deadlines.
   The AI responds in Russian and Kazakh. It's not a translated foreign product.

4. AI THAT KNOWS YOUR DATA — the chatbot doesn't give generic advice.
   It says "You're in grade 10, your deadline for the DAAD scholarship
   is in 3 weeks, and you haven't enrolled in the English Writing course yet."

5. MENTOR-STUDENT BRIDGE — real students and graduates from top universities
   share knowledge and post opportunities. Not just algorithm-generated content.
